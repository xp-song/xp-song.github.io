Ecosystem Services in Urban Landscapes

Jan 2016
Project website Project booklet All publications News
Bishan-Ang Mo Kio Park, Singapore

Urban greenery can help mitigate the negative impacts of urbanisation, and improve city liveability for urban dwellers. The benefits associated with greenery are realised through the provision of ‘ecosystem services’ such as evaporative cooling, rainfall interception, and green spaces for recreation.

Most research on urban ecosystem services has been conducted in temperate Europe and North America, but we know relatively little about how tropical cities can be designed to enhance ecosystem service provision. Tropical areas, particularly Southeast Asia, are rapidly becoming more urban, with cities growing larger and people living in higher densities. It is therefore important to understand how urban greenery can contribute to enhancing the sustainability and resilience of such cities.

This project uses new types of data and modelling approaches to understand how we can improve the ecological performance of urban greenery. Physical, ecological and socio-cultural parameters are used as inputs to model various types of ecosystem services. Landscape design is performed using 3D point cloud data, to achieve highly accurate representations of existing landscapes. The iterative process of model simulation and design feedback provides opportunities to improve designs based on scientific results.

The project was funded from 2016–2020 by the Singapore National Research Foundation ($2,256,550), awarded to the Future Cities Laboratory, Singapore-ETH Centre. The lead principal investigator was Prof. Peter Edwards.
