Data-driven approaches to understand human interactions with the environment

Apr 2018
News Code

People’s experiences of their surroundings can vary depending on where they are and their personal interests. Today, the internet and geo-located social media have opened up new ways for us to understand these differences. Technologies to interpret text and image content have also been improving, which can be used to assess the effects of the environment on people’s behaviour, sentiment and aesthetic experiences at specific places.

Many studies have used geo-located social media as a proxy for outdoor recreation, but fewer have examined how experiences of a place vary between different groups of people. Such data are often generated bottom-up, also making it challenging to ensure that all people groups are properly represented. There are thus opportunities to improve the reliability of these data-driven approaches, and build integrated workflows that consider both the effects of ‘place’ and ‘people’ across multiple datasets.
