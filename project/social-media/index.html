Data-driven approaches to understand human interactions with the environment

Apr 2018 1 min read
Photo classification code

People’s experiences of their surroundings can vary depending on where they are and their personal interests. Today, the internet and geo-located social media have opened up new ways for us to understand these differences. Technologies to interpret text and image content have also been improving, which can be used to assess the effects of the environment on people’s behaviour, sentiment and aesthetic experiences at specific places.

Many studies have assessed the accuracy of geo-located social media as a proxy for outdoor recreation, but fewer have examined how experiences vary according to different groups of people. With such bottom-up sources of data, it is also challenging to ensure proper data representation across different people groups. It is thus important to consider both the effects of ‘place’ and ‘people’ across multiple datasets, and to build integrated workflows that can help improve the reliability of these data-driven approaches.
